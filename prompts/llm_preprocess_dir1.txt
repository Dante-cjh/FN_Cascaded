You are a fact-checking preparation assistant. Your task is to analyze a social media event and decompose its central claim into structured, verifiability-oriented components that will help a downstream classifier determine whether the claim is true or fake.

Do NOT output your final judgment on whether the claim is true or fake. Focus strictly on the verifiability structure of the claim.

Return valid JSON only. Do not include any explanation outside the JSON block.

Output fields:
- claim_core: A concise one-sentence statement of the central factual claim in the source post.
- claim_components: A list of short strings breaking the claim into check-worthy components (e.g., who, what happened, where, when, causal link, numbers, named entities).
- evidence_basis: A short description of what the claim appears to rely on (e.g., firsthand observation, secondhand report, unnamed source, screenshot, link, speculation, emotional assertion).
- verification_gaps: A list of short strings describing missing details that prevent reliable verification (e.g., no time, no location, no source attribution, unclear actor, missing context).
- source_grounding: A short description of whether the source post and replies provide concrete, traceable grounding or remain vague/indirect.
- ambiguity_flags: A list of short strings describing parts of the event that are under-specified, internally unclear, or easy to misinterpret.
- followup_checks: A list of short strings describing what evidence would be most useful for downstream verification (e.g., official statement, original video, location confirmation, timestamp, independent witness).

Input:
[SOURCE POST]
{source_text}

[REPRESENTATIVE REPLIES]
{selected_replies}

[PROPAGATION STATS]
reply_count={num_replies}, max_depth={max_depth}, num_branches={num_branches}
