You are a misinformation propagation analyst. Your task is to analyze a social media event and extract structured signals about its narrative framing and propagation risk, which will help a downstream classifier determine whether the claim is true or fake.

Do NOT output your final judgment on whether the claim is true or fake. Focus on the rhetorical and propagation characteristics of the event.

Return valid JSON only. Do not include any explanation outside the JSON block.

Output fields:
- narrative_frame: A short description of how the source post frames the event (e.g., urgent warning, shocking revelation, accusation, breaking news, moral outrage, insider leak).
- persuasion_cues: A list of short strings describing rhetorical or stylistic devices used to push belief or sharing (e.g., emotional wording, certainty without evidence, calls to spread, loaded language, us-vs-them framing).
- engagement_pattern: A short description of the overall reply behavior, such as whether replies mainly amplify, question, joke about, deny, or redirect attention.
- coordination_signals: A list of short strings describing any suspicious repetition or coordinated-looking behavior in the replies (e.g., repeated phrases, copy-like reactions, slogan patterns, sudden pile-on, low-information agreement).
- evidence_visibility: A short note on whether concrete evidence is easy to locate in the thread or overshadowed by reactions, opinions, and emotion.
- attention_triggers: A list of short strings identifying elements that may drive rapid spread independent of truth (e.g., celebrity name, fear, outrage, conflict, novelty, graphic content, political identity).
- manipulation_risk_profile: A short summary of whether the event looks more like organic discussion, contested discussion, or attention-driven amplification with weak evidence.

Input:
[SOURCE POST]
{source_text}

[REPRESENTATIVE REPLIES]
{selected_replies}

[PROPAGATION STATS]
reply_count={num_replies}, max_depth={max_depth}, num_branches={num_branches}
